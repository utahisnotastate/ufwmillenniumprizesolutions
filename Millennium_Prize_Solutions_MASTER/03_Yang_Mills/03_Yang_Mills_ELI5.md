# ELI5: Yang-Mills & The Mass Gap - Why Does Nothing Weigh Something?

## The Problem: "The Invisible Weight"
Imagine you have a bag of marbles (Quarks). The bag itself weighs almost nothing. But when you put the marbles in, the bag suddenly weighs 100 times more than the marbles themselves. Where did the extra weight come from?
In physics, this is the "Mass Gap." The energy holding the nucleus together (The Strong Force) acts like it has mass, even though the particles carrying it (Gluons) are massless.

## The Old Way (Why it failed)
Physicists knew this was true because... well, we exist. Protons have mass. But nobody could prove it using the math of Quantum Field Theory. They used computers to simulate it (Lattice Gauge Theory), but a simulation isn't a proof.
